My Fears: If I take it, I'll burn out again like I did two years ago. If I don't take it, I'll regret missing the opportunity and wonder if I'm playing it too safe. The fear of making the wrong choice is paralyzing.

Writing Through the Options:

Option A: Take the promotion. Pros: More money, recognition, career advancement. Cons: Less time for family, hobbies, self-care. Higher stress. Risk of burnout.

Option B: Decline the promotion. Pros: Maintain work-life balance, time for what matters. Cons: Stagnation, less money, might regret not seizing opportunity.

The Decision: I'm going to decline the promotion. Instead, I'll negotiate for a raise in my current role and use the extra time to pursue the creative projects I've been neglecting. Financial security matters, but not at the cost of my soul.

Learning Something New: The Beginner's Mind

Learning Excited
March 6, 2024 - 3:30 PM

I started learning Spanish today, and it's humbling to be a complete beginner again. At work, I'm the expert people come to for answers. In my Spanish class, I can barely pronounce "hola" correctly.

But there's something liberating about being a beginner. The pressure to be perfect is gone because perfection isn't expected. I can make mistakes, ask "stupid" questions, and be completely awkward without it affecting my professional reputation.

Today I learned that Spanish has two ways to say "to be" - ser and estar - and they're used in completely different contexts. My brain struggled to grasp this concept that seems so natural to native speakers. I felt frustrated, then embarrassed, then just laughed at myself.

It's been years since I was truly bad at something I cared about learning. I think I've been avoiding this feeling - choosing to stay in my comfort zone where I'm competent and confident. But growth only happens at the edges of comfort.

My teacher said something that stuck with me: "In Spanish, we don't say 'I made a mistake.' We say 'I learned.'" What a beautiful reframing. Mistakes aren't failures - they're data points in the learning process.

I'm going to embrace being a beginner. It's uncomfortable, yes, but it's also where the magic happens. Who knows what else I might discover about myself when I'm willing to be bad at something new?

Sentiment: Growth-oriented - Embraces discomfort as part of learning

Key Themes: Learning, growth mindset, humility, stepping outside comfort zone
